North Coimbatore

Suriya Fans Club

Our Journey

FC Timeline

2011 – Present

Pre-2011
The Roots
Members were already involved in charity work and blood donation camps, showing dedication to social service inspired by Suriya — long before the official formation.
2011
Official Formation
Our Fans Club was officially established on 25.10.2011. First-ever FDFS celebration as a registered fan club was for the movie 7aum Arivu.
2012
Education & Expansion
Education support, welfare activities, and grand FDFS celebrations — along with early club expansion across the region.
2013
Large-Scale Welfare Begins
The club launched large-scale welfare activities including blood donation camps and charity programs, cementing its welfare-first identity.
2014
Massive Celebrations & Growth
New divisions formed, fan club boards opened citywide, and we organized the biggest-ever FDFS in Coimbatore with 5+ shows — one of the largest crowd gatherings for any actor in the city.
2015
Monthly Welfare Ritual
Fans began conducting special welfare events on the 23rd of every month to honour Suriya's birthday month — a tradition born from devotion.
2016
Mega Welfare & Online Presence
Large welfare initiatives conducted and activities started being shared online, strengthening unity among fans across the region.
2017
Community Support Programs
Members organised food distribution programs, charity visits, and fan meet gatherings — deepening the club's roots in the community.
2018
Education Support Initiative
Inspired by Agaram Foundation, the club supported the education needs of underprivileged students across Coimbatore.
2019
Digital Fan Community
Fan updates and activities migrated to WhatsApp groups and social media platforms, connecting more fans online than ever before.
2020
COVID-19 Relief
During the pandemic, members stepped up — distributing food kits and essential supplies to communities in need across Coimbatore.
2021
Fan Support Initiative
The club extended a helping hand to financially struggling fan club members who were severely affected by the pandemic.
2022
Post-Pandemic Theatre Celebrations
Grand FDFS for Etharkkum Thunindhavan as theatres reopened. The OSFC – Coimbatore team was also launched as a powerful online fan community.
2023
Expanding the Network
More than 50 new fan club boards opened across Coimbatore. OSFC Coimbatore also conducted its first welfare initiative — a summer camp program.
2024
Birthday Celebrations & Suriya Meets Us
Massive welfare initiatives during Suriya's birthday. During the shooting of Karuppu in Coimbatore, Suriya met our members for a special photo session.
2025
Youth Wing Expansion
15+ welfare activities during Suriya's birthday month. Youth Wing expanded with 300+ new members. Grand Annadhanam for 25,000+ devotees on the occasion of Koniyamman Ther Festival.
2026
15+ Years of Unity
Celebrating over 15 years of unity — continuing fan unity, social welfare, and support inspired by Suriya's values. Grand Annadhanam for 15,000+ devotees on Koniyamman Ther Festival.

Founder - Suriya's Message

A Society Where Everyone
Stands on Equal Ground.

“

Our dream is simple: A society where everyone stands on equal ground. I call it simple because something this fundamental should be, but the reality is far from it. That's why our fight is about more than good intentions; it's about building a world where fairness isn't a privilege.

Not because luck favoured them, but because opportunity did. And the surest way we know to get there is through education. Why education? Because it doesn't just change a child's report card, it changes their horizon. It passes on the tools, the values, and the confidence to step into the world on their terms.

Give it to one child, and you set in motion a ripple, a butterfly effect that shapes not just their life, but entire generations to come. At Agaram, this is the heartbeat of our work. Not just handing out degrees, but cultivating futures capable of carrying the weight of a fairer tomorrow — for decades, for lifetimes.

And we are doing it shoulder-to-shoulder with volunteers, well-wishers, and fellow dreamers, each adding their own thread to this fabric of change in society. An equal society isn't a finished portrait; we have added our strokes, but the canvas still calls for many more hands, and that's what we are working to bring together.

Suriya
